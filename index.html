Profile

About.

Drew Hull is the Principal and Co-Founder of Jefferson & Associates LLC, a Dallas-based regulatory strategy and government affairs firm he runs with his wife and co-founder, Paige Monet Brown. He represents cannabis, hemp, and other regulated-industry clients in the rooms where policy is actually written — legislatures, executive agencies, and the rulemaking dockets that turn statute into operating reality.

Before founding the firm, Drew spent five years leading government relations at True Terpenes, where he moved from advocacy into authorship. He drafted regulatory language adopted into the operative cannabis frameworks of two of the largest U.S. markets — California's Department of Cannabis Control (4 CCR div. 19) and New York's Office of Cannabis Management under the Marihuana Regulation and Taxation Act (N.Y. Cannabis Law arts. 4–5; 9 NYCRR).

His practice is built on statutory fluency — the federal definition of hemp under the 2018 Farm Bill (Pub. L. 115-334; 7 U.S.C. §§ 1639o–1639s), the Controlled Substances Act schedules (21 C.F.R. pt. 1308), the § 280E tax wall (26 U.S.C. § 280E), and the DEA's evolving cannabinoid interpretations (85 Fed. Reg. 51,639). He pairs that technical command with coalition and trade-association strategy across multiple states, building the legislative and organizational infrastructure that gives a fragmented industry one credible voice.

A lifelong competitive debater — he competed in policy (CX) debate and went on to coach at the high-school and collegiate levels — Drew approaches advocacy the way he approached a round: evidence first, argument structured, nothing asserted that can't be cited. Based in Dallas, Texas, he is married to Jefferson & Associates co-founder Paige Monet Brown.

What I Do

Expertise.

I

Regulatory Drafting & Rulemaking

Translating policy goals into adoptable text — comment letters, redlines, and statutory and regulatory language engineered to survive agency review and litigation rather than just register an opinion.

Authority — Cal. Code Regs. tit. 4, div. 19 (DCC); N.Y. Cannabis Law arts. 4–5 & 9 NYCRR (OCM); 5 U.S.C. § 553 (APA notice-and-comment).

II

Federal Hemp & Farm Bill Policy

Directing legislative strategy on the federal definition of hemp and total-THC standards, and tracking House and Senate Agriculture Committee activity through the Farm Bill reauthorization and appropriations cycle.

Authority — Agriculture Improvement Act of 2018, Pub. L. 115-334; 7 U.S.C. §§ 1639o–1639s.

III

Controlled Substances & DEA Scheduling

Counsel on cannabinoid scheduling, chemical-conversion interpretive positions, and the procedural posture of DEA rulemaking and administrative proceedings — where the fight is often the process, not just the policy.

Authority — Controlled Substances Act, 21 U.S.C. § 801 et seq.; 21 C.F.R. pt. 1308; 85 Fed. Reg. 51,639 (DEA 2020 IFR).

IV

Government Affairs & Lobbying

Direct engagement with Congress, executive-branch staff, and state legislatures — converting political risk into a drafted, defensible position across Texas, Oregon, Ohio, Florida, Illinois, Pennsylvania, and New York.

Authority — Lobbying Disclosure Act, 2 U.S.C. §§ 1601–1614; applicable state lobbyist-registration codes.

V

Coalition & Trade Association Strategy

Formation, governance, and legislative programming for trade associations and advocacy coalitions — the durable infrastructure that lets an industry speak with one credible, sustained voice.

Authority — I.R.C. § 501(c)(6); lobbying-expense treatment under 26 U.S.C. § 162(e).

VI

Tax & Compliance Strategy

Navigating the structural tax and compliance burdens unique to a federally controlled-substance-adjacent industry — from the § 280E deduction wall to state seed-to-sale track-and-trace.

Authority — 26 U.S.C. § 280E; 26 U.S.C. § 471 (cost of goods sold); state track-and-trace regs.
